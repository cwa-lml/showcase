Tension

The conversation needs to be set up with a purpose and the more tension that can be introduced the more the students will have to work with. For example, giving bad news, an argument about an issue, an understanding barrier (language, ability, memory). It will be the tension that makes the drama, makes it memorable for the students and hooks them in to the learning. There are a variety of ways to add tension:

  • Time – put a time limit on the task; There are 10 minutes to convey the information. The decision must be made in 10 minutes, as the plane is leaving and the other person has to be on it. If they don't take their medication soon they may become seriously ill.
  • Competition for resources – If they take all the money there will be none left for you.
  • Life threatening situations for the characters.
  • Risk for the environment.
