Level 3: Improvisation – Different Expectations

ELEMENTS/TECHNIQUES/CONVENTIONS

Elements

  • Role

    A variety of roles is possible in this learning example, so that students can explore a range of opinions, attitudes, expectations, and points-of-view.

  • Tension

    In taking on different roles, the students will reshape conversations and role plays to reflect different perspectives and changing tensions.

  • Focus

    In this case the focus is the issue being dealt with in the drama and the different resolutions that may be possible. The students in the drama have the chance to reflect, justify, present an argument, and come to a broader understanding of an issue. They are not expected to reach a common resolution.

Techniques

  • Voice

    Students will be speaking from role. Because of the nature of the content, it is likely to generate argument and heated discussion. Several strategies may be used to focus students' listening and to ensure that their role plays are productive

    Try the following strategies:

    • One person speaking at a time, with no interruption. Ensure that the student indicates their intention by raising their hand. Teacher takes all contributions and indicates speaking order to students.
    • One person speaking at a time, as above, but this time, with interruptions which are not acknowledged.
    • One person speaking at a time as above, but also making an effort to listen and give every opinion a chance to be aired. After discussion evaluate responses, script them, or present them as taped conversations or as live role play.
    • Teacher may wish to act as recorder of information and appoint a student to chair order of speaking so that reference material is available for students to construct a script. They may begin by creating a 10-line dialogue and then extend on it.

Conventions

  • Role play (between the people involved).
  • Soundscape. [tip]
  • A sound picture could be made by the class, with everyone contributing a short line/ sound/ sentence expressing a range of opinions about the issue to be compiled as a running sound track. This could be recorded on audiotape.
  • Telephone conversation. [tip]
  • Between two people involved in sharing opinions or reporting on a game, for example, two parents, coaches of opposing teams, a brother or a sister, teacher or principal, sideline supporters.
