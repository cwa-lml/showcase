  • Gather a wide stylistic collection of originals and arrangements
  • Use both teacher-directed and more student-centred approaches (eg. research in pairs/small groups) while listening to music examples, playbacks and experimentation with effects
  • Encourage students to find own examples of versions of music they are listening to or practising
  • Provide enough tuned instruments for class work. If available, encourage use of computer music programs like Band in a Box. Composer Notes/Arranging section.
  • Provide tape recorders and encourage students to record their ideas frequently.
  • Read and use a range of texts to support your teaching programme such as Busker's books, Fake It books, composition texts (go to Teaching and Learning Materials)
