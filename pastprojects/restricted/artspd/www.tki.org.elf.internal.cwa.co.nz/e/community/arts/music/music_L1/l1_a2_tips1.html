Rhythm is easiest for students with voice, then with hands and body percussion, then with instruments, last with feet in locomotor or whole-body movement
Play tapes with rhythmic music and have the class keep the beat with their fingers on their knees. Suitable tapes are: Te Vaka, Mozart, Vivaldi's four seasons, Moana and the Moa-Hunters' Tahi Make sure you can offer a range of sounds - practice!
You may find it helpful to use a tape with a steady beat to keep everyone moving, or play a tambourine or a drum.
Provide a range of untuned percussion sounds: light sticks, wood blocks, scrapers, metal sounds, drums of various kinds including homemade.
Keep the rhythm going and move at a good pace to keep students energised. Bring in the voice, try consonants (unvoiced): P P T T/ P T K K
Consider starting from a voice or chant
Introduce tuned percussion only when everything else is explored fully. Then consider glockenspiels, chime bars, xylophones, keyboards.
If you have glockenspiels, consider using only the black notes to start with, which sound pleasant together, even when the entire class is responding at the same time with different patterns
