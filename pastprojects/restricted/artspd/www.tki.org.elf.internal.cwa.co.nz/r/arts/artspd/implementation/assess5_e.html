Assessment and Reporting

The Influence of the Curriculum on Arts Assessment

Along with the potential to enrich teaching and learning in the arts the new curriculum document for the arts has, for the first time, provided teachers of the arts with valid national achievement objectives around which to structure their teaching.

It will be necessary for teachers to have an understanding of the meaning and intent of the strands so they can confidently teach and evaluate learning programmes in each discipline.

Exemplars

With the development of national exemplars now underway, teachers will have a better awareness of the developmental steps involved in learning in, through, and about dance, drama, music and visual arts. They will be able to pitch their expectations realistically for their own classrooms, by using richly annotated examples which will illustrate learning, achievement, and quality in relation to curriculum levels. They will signal important things for teachers to watch for in each arts discipline.

Resources

Dance
A video resource pack comprising two dance videos, one for years 1 to 6 and one for years 7 to 10 plus accompanying Teachers Notes are currently under development. This resource should be in schools early in 2002. Other video and print-based resources have also been proposed and are at various stages of development. Further information will be provided in future updates.

Drama
All schools will have received a copy of the Drama in the Classroom video and accompanying booklet. This resource looks specifically at the elements, techniques, and conventions of drama. In two parts, it is aimed at years 1 to 10. Further resources are planned for 2002.

Music
The Music Handbooks, which supported the old syllabus document, are currently being revised. The first to be completed is the Junior Music Handbook and CD which has been very successfully trialled in a number of schools. These will be supplied to schools by 2002. Other handbooks and Planning and Assessment Guidelines, all published by Learning Media are proposed for 2002 and 2003.

Sounz (The Centre for New Zealand Music) is publishing a resource called Ears Wide Open written by Cheryl Camm and that will be available for schools to purchase from term 3 2001. This resource has a wealth of teacher guidelines and student copymaster activities, plus CD, for levels 1 to 4 of the music curriculum. It is based entirely on a wide range of contemporary New Zealand music.

Other resources are being be published by various groups for primary and secondary schools to support Music teaching and learning. This includes many more learning examples uploaded onto the tki website www.tki.org.nz/arts

Visual Arts
Five visual arts resource packs, consisting of books with posters, will be in schools by the end of 2001. The Painting and Sculpture packs have already been posted to all schools. Schools will be provided with one set for every five teachers at no charge. (Contact Learning Media for more.) Printmaking will be available from the end of June, and the Fabric and Fibre and Design resources will be available in term 4. Further resources will be written during 2002.
