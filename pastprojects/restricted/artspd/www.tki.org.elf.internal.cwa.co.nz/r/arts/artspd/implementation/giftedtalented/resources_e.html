Ministry of Education resources

Gifted and Talented Students Meeting Their Needs in New Zealand Schools (Ministry of Education, 2000) is a useful book, and can be found in schools. It contains lists of characteristics of gifted/talented students on pages 18–19.

Parents play an important role in identifying gifted/talented children and can give information about their child's activities at home that illustrate the characteristics listed on these pages.

For example, the characteristics, intellectual playfulness, imagination, and fantasy are mentioned under creative thinking. Children often engage in fantasy drawings about imagined happenings. These often include characters from youth culture and television. Children create these drawings for themselves, not an audience. They are not like school art (Golomb, 1992, p. 202) and the teacher may not see evidence of this at school.
