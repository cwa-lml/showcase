Evaluation/Reflection

Students were asked:

  • what they found difficult;


  • what they most enjoyed;


  • how they would like the unit changed;


  • whether they would consider a career as a ceramic artist.


Georgia asked herself:

  • whether the overall timeframe allowed students to develop their design ideas;


  • whether students had more understanding of Niuean culture because of their experimentation;


  • whether the unit enabled students to express themselves in meaningful, creative, and imaginative ways.


Where to next?

  • More opportunities to comment on designs and motifs from other cultures.


  • Build up a resource from the material students found (on the web or photocopied from books) for use in the next unit.


  • Keep teaching and reinforcing an arts vocabulary (i.e., form, texture, elasticity, pliant, bisque, glaze) and techniques for making artworks.
They could see that the clay had gone from soft and pliant, to hard.

It had changed in the firing.

The glossy glaze made it look professional – "proper".

They were pleased with their efforts.

Georgia Vaughan
