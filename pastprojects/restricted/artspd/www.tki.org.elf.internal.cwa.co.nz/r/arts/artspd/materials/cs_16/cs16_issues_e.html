Establishing a School of Arts at Tauranga Boys' College

Introduction | Organisation/Planning | Content | Issues | Evaluation/Reflection

Issues

The strategic planning identified several key issues for the school to resolve. The criteria were to:

  • provide curriculum pathways within the arts
  • promote values and excellence
  • encourage all students to value their cultural heritage
  • make use of community resources, and broaden the range of opportunities/activities provided
  • encourage and support creativity
  • advance learning in the arts by making inter-disciplinary activities accessible to all students.

The School of Arts at Tauranga Boys' College is now past the development and implementation phases, and evaluating success in meeting their criteria.

More specific issues were identified by teachers at the school as follows:

  • Budget issues have been the predominant focus for Tauranga Boys' College as they continue to raise funds beyond the $1,000,000 currently achieved in order to reach the completion date of the second stage in 2005.
  • Currently dance is not taught as an arts discipline across the school. It is experienced largely through kapahaka classes and as a special project to support the school production. When the dance and drama Youth Theatre centre is fully established, dance is intended to be looked at as a whole subject in the performing arts faculty. The school realises the need to actively seek to appoint such a suitable person in the near future.
  • The arts timetable for senior students is to be redesigned in 2005 for 2006 so that students can do multiple arts courses, and combined arts courses. This involves team teaching, shared curriculum timetable lines, shared resources, and collaborative operational systems to be established.
  • There is a desire to have an arts resource centre where all 'departments' store and share resources, in addition to the necessary individual spaces designed for the requirements of each discipline for example, soundproofed practice rooms for music, dedicated technology laboratories for music and visual arts, drama and dance rehearsal spaces.
