Producing Rich Digital Resources for classroom use

The Problem




B.Y.O.D

Bring Your Own Device

  • Schools cannot afford IT for every student
  • Therefore, they are resorting to letting students bring their own devices
  • Their are often guidelines around allowed devices and usage
  • However, these differ from school to school

BYOD: the experience

Useful feedback from Orewa College:
  • All year 9 students
  • Recommended iPad 2
  • Battery life important: should last entire school day
  • Student performance compared to last year slightly better, noticeably boys more than girls
  • Hard to ascribe directly to BYOD, but importantly, no harm is being done
  • Now rolling out to years 8-10 in 2013, and undertaking staff PD using iPads.

BYOD: The Challenge for LML

How do we produce digital content that works across devices?

  • Engaging:  implies interactivity and animation for at least some resources
  • Cost-effective to produce, even though it is supposed to work on a wide-range of screen-sizes and OSes
  • Easily-delivered:   must be easy for students to download and install

BYOD: The Challenge for LML

  • Native app solutions can be wonderful, but also costly
  • Web solutions can be wide-reaching, but we also need offline
  • App frameworks such as DPS can be wonderful and relatively cheap, however only work on certain platforms

WEB Technologies

  • HTML/CSS/JS
  • Can be delivered via the browser
  • Can be wrapped into apps (for offline use)
  • Most devices have a browser
  • Schools espousing BYOD have been ensuring their network is up to scratch
  • Allows relatively easy distribution

Web Technologies: Issues

  • 10 years behind Flash?
  • Tools for rich content production are still evolving
  • Standards (HTML5/CSS3 etc) and implementation are still evolving

Web Technologies: Questions

  • Are there tools that we can use?
  • Do we have enough skills in-house?
  • Do we understand the user requirements?
  • Can we produce content that is rich enough, but also cheap enough?
