Gather a range of sound sources

Provide ongoing opportunities to experiment with contrast in music through singing, playing, moving and listening.

Provide instruments that demonstrate contrasting timbres, for example, skin sounds, metal sounds, wood sounds, string sounds, shakers. Consider also natural materials such as flax, stones, sticks, bamboo, shells, and home-made/class-made instruments

Model contrast with sound using a range of sound sources.

Provide ongoing opportunities for students to explore sound sources individually and in small groups.

Seek out different physical spaces for sound exploration to occur.

Establish with students and colleagues accepted safe practices (personal, physical space, use of and care for instruments)

Look at graphically notated scores of composers. Listen to recordings of these pieces of music. For example, Singing Door and Voice Piece (Our Music), Iron Lung (Ears Wide Open). Use these ideas and symbols that surround us in our everyday lives as a basis for motivating the children's own pictorial representations.
