The telephone conversation could be in pairs – where both sides of the conversation are heard or a one way conversation where the group only hears one side. The latter leaves the way open for:

  • Journal entries of the other side
  • Improvisation of the other side
  • Teacher questioning students about what they felt might have been said. Some warm up exercises for this:
    • One person repeats the same sentence using a variety of inflections for example, "If you would just listen to what I have to say." This can be done in pairs or each participant can speak in front of the whole group. A lot can be learnt about how people say things and what "presses buttons" for people.
    • In pairs, each person is given one sentence that they have to repeat while the rest of the group watches on for example, "Please stay," "What is there to stay for?"
