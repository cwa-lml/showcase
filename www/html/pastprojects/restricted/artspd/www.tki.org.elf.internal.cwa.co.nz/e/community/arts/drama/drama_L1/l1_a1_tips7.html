Setting the context

Never underestimate the importance of setting the context. The more thorough it is, the more the students will engage in the drama.

A real cake, a wrapped present, the discussion of what would be appropriate for the present. Start to show the students the possibilities, asking them to tell about similar events in their lives will also help build the belief.

If another character is to be introduced make sure the students know many details about this person, give them a clear biography – not just the details relevant for the drama. (See it as an iceberg – you need the tip that is above water, the belief building process is giving them some of the ice that lies below the surface of the water.)

Always draw analogies with things that the students can relate to. In setting the context it is important to begin with statements like the following:

Today we are going to make a play about a husband and wife who are celebrating their golden anniversary.
