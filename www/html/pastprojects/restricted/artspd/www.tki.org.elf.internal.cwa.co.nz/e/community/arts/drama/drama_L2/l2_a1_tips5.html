Discovering the object

  • Consider hiding the object in the school.
  • The discovery could even start the drama (particularly with students who have had some experience in working in this way).
  • A suitcase could be sitting in the middle of the room when the students return from the lunchbreak. The teacher could move straight into a low status role and question the students about what they think the object signifies.
  • Things that are ripped and need piecing together add to the excitement and learning opportunities; but only if it fits the context of the drama. A letter should only be ripped if there is a reason, for example, the reader did not like the content, or had decided not to send it.
