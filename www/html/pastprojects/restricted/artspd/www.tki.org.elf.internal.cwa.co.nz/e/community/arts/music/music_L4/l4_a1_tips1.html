As a teacher:

  • Gather a range of sound sources
  • Provide a quantity of one particular sound source
  • Provide ongoing opportunities for students to explore sound sources individually and in small groups
  • Seek out a range of different physical spaces for sound experimentation so students can do quality listening to own work
  • Encourage experimentation of a wide variety of symbolic representation of sound. Draw on individual students' prior knowledge (perhaps of conventional notation or ability to produce creative graphic representation) and share actual composers' own scores as a source of motivation eg from Our Music and Ears Wide Open resources (Teaching and Learning Material)
  • Provide portable tape machines with built-in microphones for easy recording of sound-making and encourage students to help each other with recording process
