  • The symbolic representations of sound is useful for hearing impaired students. They like to be able to "see" what they are going to play.
  • Use visual cues whenever possible. Have pictures of instruments and found sounds; use storybooks, and videos.
  • Work in very small groups whenever possible; a teacher aid might take the student(s) who has hearing loss in a separate group from the class at times.
  • Each new sound needs to be heard on its own. Students who are hearing impaired have difficulty discriminating sounds, and picking out a specific sound from others in the environment, so eliminate background noise as much as possible.
  • When working in a group, seat the students in a circle so the child or children who have hearing impairment, can see other people's faces.
  • Allow students who are hearing impaired to have lots of hands on experimentation with sound.
  • To ensure each task is understood, give examples before asking for ideas. If another student offers an idea, make sure everyone in the group has heard and understood what was said.
