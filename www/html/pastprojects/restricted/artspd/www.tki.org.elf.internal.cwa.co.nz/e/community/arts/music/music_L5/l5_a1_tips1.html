  • Gather a wide stylistic collection of music that provides clear examples of the use of motif, sequence, repetition, variation.
  • Use both teacher-directed and more student-centered approaches (eg. research in pairs/small groups) while listening to music examples
  • Encourage students to find own examples of the use of music structural devices within music they are listening to or practising
  • Provide enough tuned instruments for class work. If available, encourage use of computer music programs like Logic, Composer Notes or Sibelius. These will easily allow students to return to their compositions and refine them over time.
  • Provide tape recorders and encourage students to record their ideas frequently.
  • Read and use Dorothy Buchanan's "To Dream Out Loud" , "Composing in the Classroom", "Listen, Compose, Perform", "From Scratch:, "Our Music", "Ears Wide Open" text resources.
