Some conventions work well with exploring the tensions:

  • Chorus – a group of chanters, singers, or rappers portrays information about the turmoil the character is experiencing.
  • Consciences arguing – the devil/angel sides of the personality argue about the predicament. They may stand behind the person – one on each shoulder.
  • Voices in the head – like voice-overs or asides. The rest of the characters might move into freeze frame while the person in conflict comes to the front and voices their inner turmoil. The teacher may also freeze the action and come to the different students in role – tap them on the shoulder and ask them to speak what is in their head. This can sometimes tap into the experience in a way that might not come out if the drama carries on.
