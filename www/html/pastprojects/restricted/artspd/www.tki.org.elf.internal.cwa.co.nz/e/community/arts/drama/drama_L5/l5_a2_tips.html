At every opportunity in this work teachers need to encourage students to create full characters and to avoid stereotypes and one dimensional figures. Challenging students to think beyond the obvious will provide a richer basis for the investigative work and the presentations. In the initial character creation work students need to select and create objects of significance that can suggest an event or a part of a person's history. Keep the focus of this initial work on the objects rather than on the artistic process of making them. Set a clear time limit on finding and displaying the objects. This will encourage students to be focused and disciplined in their work. In the presentation stage focus on the similarities and differences between the initial character and the findings of the investigative group. Encourage students to see the richness that is possible in different interpretations of the same material.
