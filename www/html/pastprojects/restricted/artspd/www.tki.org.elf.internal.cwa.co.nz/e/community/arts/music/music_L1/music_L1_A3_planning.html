Level 1: Adapting Action Songs and Chants

PLANNING AND ASSESSMENT

Key teaching and learning concepts

Students will experience:

Action Songs: Songs that are accompanied by gestures and actions. Actions can range from small hand-and finger plays (e.g. Little Mousie Brown), to whole body movements and larger gestures (e.g., Look at me Jumping).

Chants: Rhythmic spoken verses. They often accompany movement. For example, skipping chants like: Wash the dishes, dry the dishes, turn the dishes over.

In both Action Songs and chants there will be an underlying structure of regular rhythm beats over which the students will chant.

The elements of music which provide the tools to adapt and change existing music

  • Tempo: changes in the speed of the music will affect its mood.
  • Dynamics: loud and soft can develop into getting louder (crescendo) and getting softer (decrescendo). Introduce these symbols:

Prior learning

Singing and chanting rhymes (Fuzzy Wuzzy Was a Bear), raps (Copycat Rap), times tables (if appropriate), nursery rhymes (Humpty Dumpty), playground chants (I'm the king of the castle) and games (What's the time Mister Wolf?).

Suggested learning outcomes

The students will demonstrate the ability to:

  • perform chants from different cultures, by memory (CI, UC);
  • vary the mood of a chant by changing dynamics and tempo (PK);
  • compose their own chant individually or contribute to a group composition, drawing on their personal experience and imagination (DI);
  • perform their original chant to others (CI).

Ideas for activities

  1. Voice work

    Try saying one phrase in many different ways: for example, "I'm not scared", I'm hungry", "Give me back my skateboard/kitten/ball".

  2. Rhythm warm-ups

    Lead the students, or have a student lead the class in echo patterns using body percussion and whole-body stepping the beat. Start with simple beat and develop by adding in voices counting, or clapping.

  3. Listen to and perform existing chants

    • Boom chick-a Boom
    • Ki ana au pakia
    • I Had a good job
    • I Left My Wife
    • I'm Hungry
    • Name chants: e.g. Ellen, bombellen, stickalellen fifellen, fifellen, stickalellen, bobellen spells Ellen
    • Copycat Rap (KK 4)
    • Pa'o Pa'o (Handbook)
    • 3 Bears (Hei Waiata Hei Whakakoakoa?)
    • Pease Porridge Hot
    • Fuzzy Wuzzy Was a Bear
    • My Mother Said
    • Toia Mai


  4. Choose one existing chant to change

    For example, Going On a Bear Hunt
    1. Change using the elements:
      Tempo – for example, slow or fast
      Dynamics, for example, loud or soft – or getting louder and getting softer (conduct with your hands)
      Rhythm – add body percussion or instrumental sounds to heighten the effectiveness of the piece.
    2. Use repeated words or phrases as an ostinato accompaniment e.g., "No, you can't" under My Mother Said.
    3. Discuss the differences in the mood, for example, cheeky, cross, when the tempo or dynamics are changed. Enhance this with a change of vocal style (say it smoothly, jerkily, in strict rhythm, growl, whisper, clip the words short).


  5. Create a class chant

    Brainstorm a new chant based around current issues, health and safety, or new learning, for example:
    • hats on outside
    • crossing the road
    • times tables
    • playground rubbish
    • days of the week in te reo Māori


    Develop your chant by:
    • adding a "chorus" or using instruments to accompany: consider body percussion, found sounds, vocal sounds;
    • Students decide on performance elements in groups: when to be loud, when to be soft, fast or slow, jerky or smooth. Write these performance indications into the written text.

    "Ask"

    What happens when we try saying the rhymes faster? Slower?
    How can we change our voices to make the performance different?
    How have you used the elements to change your chant?

[tip]

Assessment approaches

Formative

Give instant verbal feedback to the students on their performances, noting use of the elements and naming them to the children. ("That volume change from loud to soft really made me listen and felt spooky")

Use the "ask" questions in formative assessment

  • Learning Outcomes-Related Assessment Criteria

Comment on the ability of the student(s) to:

  • perform words accurately in rhythm;

  • use the elements (tempo, dynamics, rhythm and pitch where there is a chorus), in re-creating their chosen chants with changes;

  • describe how their changes affected the mood of the chant;

  • develop new chants applying the elements of music.
