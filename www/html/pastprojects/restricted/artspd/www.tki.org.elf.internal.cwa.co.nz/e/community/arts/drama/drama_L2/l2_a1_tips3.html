Found Objects

Make these as authentic as possible – a child will not accept a picture of something as being the equivalent of that thing. Do not use a drawing or photocopy for the real thing.

To begin with, for some children, you should collect objects and prepare related roles to which they will immediately relate. Some suggestions are a broken toy, a ripped photo of a child, an empty party bag, a postcard with a simple message. Your objects may be part of drama worlds where animals can talk, toys can come alive and inanimate objects have personalities so this gives rise to a host of possibilities.
