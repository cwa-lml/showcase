Unit plan

Achievement objectives

The following achievement objectives from The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum are the level 4 music strands relevant to this unit.

  • Strand PK: Developing Practical Knowledge in Music – Students will identify through focused listening, and experiment with, a range of patterns, effects, sound qualities, and structural devices.
  • Strand DI: Developing Ideas in Music – Students use musical elements, instruments, and technologies to improvise and compose simple musical pieces
  • Strand CI: Communicating and Interpreting in Music – Students will prepare, rehearse, present, and evaluate brief music performances.

Intended learning outcomes

Students will:

  • experiment with sound and silence, using different tone colours, textures, and other elements of music (strands PK and DI)
  • * develop musical ideas and movements to reflect a source of motivation (strands PK and DI)
  • create and represent a short piece in groups (strands PK and DI)
  • * appropriately perform (play and move to) their own compositions from a graphic score (strands CI).

Extension activity

Students will:

  • * perform and evaluate another group's piece of music from their graphic score (CI).
Note: * denotes learning outcomes to be assessed

Learning activities

  • Read and discuss the School Journal (1980, Part 4, No. 1) article 'Designing a carpet'.
  • Experiment with sound sources and musical ideas to reflect and connect motivation from article to creative musical ideas.
  • Share and evaluate musical ideas with others to refine work in progress.
  • In groups, finalise composition and fill in the score grid.
  • Rehearse in groups.
  • Create non-locomotor movements to interpret the music.
  • Combine music and movement, and rehearse.
  • Perform as a group, using own musical score and movements.
  • Evaluate own and others' performances.

Extension activity

  • Perform a score written by another group.
  • Discuss interpretations, and any modifications.
  • Refine scores and performances.

Assessment

Formative assessment

  • Teacher monitors and conferences throughout creative process.
  • Students use peer conferencing to reflect on effectiveness of movements, score, and overall machine music composition ideas.
  • Video-recordings of performances are viewed to facilitate further evaluation and refinement.

Summative assessment

  • Assess the student's final performance live or from video recording.

Extension activity assessment

  • Assess the student's performance from their own and/or another group's score, either live or from video recording.

Resources

  • 'Designing a Carpet' (from School Journal 1980, Part 4, No. 1) to promote discussion on machines (e.g. repetitive and rigid movement) – see below.
  • Variety of untuned percussion instruments, and/or found sound/body percussion, for each student
  • Machine score for each student, pencils and erasers.

Requirements

Divide class into pairs of groups, with equal numbers of members (preferably 4–6) in each group of the pair.
