Establishing a School of Arts at Tauranga Boys' College

Introduction | Organisation/Planning | Content | Issues | Evaluation/Reflection

Organisation

Tauranga Boys' College has always had a strong reputation in arts education with high student attainment achieved through quality teaching. The desire to develop new facilities and programmes, came from the value and recognition the teachers gave to the multi-media world the students engage in. To do this, the Arts departments reviewed their practices, established goals, and set in place a strategic plan to achieve the School of Arts.

The strategic plan for the School of Arts

Mission
Tauranga Boys' College enriches students' cultural heritage by promoting artistic excellence and advancing learning in the arts.

Vision
A college in which artistic excellence is celebrated, encouraged, supported, and available for all. A college where all students will have an appreciation of the arts, and a desire to make a positive contribution to New Zealand's society.

Guiding values

  • Artistic work and freedom of expression are a vital part of education.
  • Participating in the arts and engaging in creative endeavour are an essential part of human development.
  • Learning in the arts makes a positive contribution to our young people's academic achievement, interpersonal skills, confidence, motivation to succeed and preparation for work.
  • Support of the arts relies on partnerships that build on existing strengths within our community.
  • Appreciation of the arts enhances our knowledge and understanding of our cultural heritage.

Planning

Firstly, the school developed their strategic plan which included a mission statement, a vision statement, guiding values for the School of Arts, key strategic issues for the school, school goals, and teaching and learning goals.

Key aspects of the strategic plan can be viewed in the statements above, while the school goals are to:

  • create clear curriculum pathways for student in the arts
  • provide arts opportunities outside of the school timetable
  • provide for cross-curricular links within the arts
  • provide a Tauranga Boys' College youth theatre
  • establish a community Arts council to fundraise and manage funds
  • maintain and develop use of community as a resource.

The implementation goals to guide teaching and student learning were developed from the school goals, the mission and vision statements, and guiding curriculum values. The implementation goals are to:

  • support arts professional development
  • improve students' learning outcomes in the arts by ensuring full implementation of The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum throughout the school
  • establish a Student Arts Council
  • make students aware of career pathways in the arts
  • broaden the range of arts curriculum opportunities/activities the school provides for all students
  • provide scholarship opportunities for arts students
  • inform staff, students and the community of arts events via newsletters and The Arts Online website
  • establish a timetable structure at years 9 and 10 that ensures appreciation of the arts for example, two arts subjects at year 9, two arts subjects at year 10.
  • Strategic planning and fundraising have been carried out over the past two years with completion of the new and refurbished facilities anticipated in another two years time. Needless to say, fundraising will continue for years to come as the multi-million dollar project costs are being met from within the school and from the wider community, with the largest cost being the building of the new Youth Theatre for Performing Arts. This complex will be a multi-purpose facility, with space for functions, displays, and visual art exhibitions.

Youth theatre

The ongoing marketing of students' classroom-produced arts works, both at school and in the community (including sales of student art works at exhibitions) keeps the School of Arts profile high and funds rolling in. Many successful fundraising events have been staged which also profile students' performances and arts works. Sponsors have been generous in their support.

School of Arts newsletters featuring successes and targets are distributed on a regular basis throughout the community. This has raised the profile of arts teaching and learning in the school and in the community.

Fundraising event

Students are represented on the school's Student Arts Council and they play a significant role in the development and implementation of the arts strategic plan.

The principal and his advisory management group are strong supporters of the School of Arts development plan and the director of the School of Arts is now a member of the Principal's Advisory Group.

School of Arts management structure

In order to restructure the arts departments into a School of Arts, the 'old' school structures for the arts needed to be reviewed and a 'new' structure articulated.

Old management structure consisted of:

  • self-contained departments with an Head of Department (HOD) responsible for that arts department and responsible for the department budget
  • programmes designed specific to the particular arts discipline within and beyond the classroom
  • departments responsible for their own promotion material
  • departmental meetings held on a regular basis.

New management structure to consist of:

  • director of Arts
  • teacher in charge of performing arts
  • teacher in charge of visual arts
  • development of cross-curricular programmes
  • activities within and outside of the classroom and outside of schools hours with greater use of the community as a resource
  • timetable structured to support the arts, and clear curriculum pathways provided
  • inter-departmental meetings held on a regular basis
  • access to additional finances to support the Arts for example, arts scholarships, new equipment, collaborative and cost-effective promotions
  • representation on the Principal's Advisory Group.
School of Arts discipline structure

The School of Arts restructured according to staffing and courses available to the school with the longer term vision of greater collaboration across courses and staff. The structure decided upon is based on two groups of learning activities represented in the various arts disciplines taught at Tauranga Boys' College:

Performing arts

  • music (including audio engineering and music technology, itinerant tuition)
  • drama
  • dance (including kapahaka)

Visual arts

  • practical art (including photography, design, art history, contemporary Māori Art)
  • e-art (multimedia/moving image, digital photography, special effects, 3- dimensional animation).

Management structure
The performing arts and visual arts faculties of the School of Arts each have one head of faculty with an overall director of the School of Arts who is responsible for all operational and coordination aspects of the Schools of Arts.

Students have representation on the school Student Arts Council and there are currently seven senior student representatives, with the longer term aim of having two students from each year level so that there is a student voice throughout the school year levels. One student (a prefect) has been elected as student director of The Arts and they lead the Student Arts Council meetings which are comprised of a student leader representing each arts discipline. These students attend the School of Arts council meetings and share in decision-making and strategic planning.
