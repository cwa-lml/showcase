Learning in Dance

Use this page to inform your teacher planning for dance.

Dance is a unique medium for learning about self and the world. It is an essential component of artistic, aesthetic, and cultural education and develops creative potential through physical, non-verbal expression.
Ministry of Education. (2002). The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum. Wellington: Learning Media, p.18.

Dance education in school programmes aims to foster student "enthusiasm as participants, creators, viewers, and critical inquirers and to develop their lifelong interest in and appreciation of dance".
Ministry of Education. (2002). The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum. Wellington: Learning Media, p.19.

Students develop dance literacy as they explore and use dance concepts and skills as a way of expressing their ideas, feelings, beliefs, and values about themselves and their cultures. The arts curriculum identifies four strands as core areas of dance literacy practice: Developing Practical Knowledge in Dance, Developing Ideas in Dance, Communicating and Interpreting in Dance, and Understanding Dance in Context. The strands are inter-related and in practice are interwoven.

Developing Practical Knowledge in Dance

Elements

  • body awareness

  • space

  • time

  • energy

  • relationships

Developing and extending movement vocabularies
Exploring and using vocabularies, processes, conventions, technologies
Learning and applying safe dance practices


Developing Ideas in Dance

  • Initiating, creating, developing, conceptualising, and refining

  • Exploring a variety of ideas from differing sources

  • Improvising

  • Making and manipulating movement material

  • Selecting and combining

  • Using a variety of choreographic:
    • structures, e.g., ABA, narrative

    • devices, e.g., repetition, inversion

    • processes, e.g., group collaboration


  • Decision-making

  • Expressing and communicating ideas, feelings, information, beliefs, experiences


Communicating and Interpreting in Dance

  • Learning, rehearsing, sharing and/or performing dance to others

  • Developing performance skills

  • Learning about and using new technologies

  • Viewing

  • Responding

  • Describing

  • Analysing

  • Interpreting

  • Evaluating

  • Reflecting

  • Making increasingly informed judgments


Understanding Dance in Context

Contextual Information
  • Identifying forms and functions - artistic, ritual, social

  • Understanding contribution and significance

  • Appreciating traditions and changes

  • Investigating and celebrating traditional Māori dance and multicultural dance heritage of New Zealand society
