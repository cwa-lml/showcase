Extension activities

Motivation sources

Soundscapes or soundtracks can be created from many sources of motivation, including:

  • poems;
  • photos;
  • stories;
  • current class topics (such as Antarctica).

Natural sounds of the environment could be used as a source of motivation for creating a soundscape. This could explore some important aspects of tikanga and te reo Māori, such as incorporating sounds of taonga puoro (traditional Māori instruments).

Students can be encouraged to create soundscapes using a music form, such as:

  • verse and chorus;
  • binary – AB;
  • ternary – ABA;
  • rondo – ABACA.

Models of soundscapes

There are many listening examples of soundscapes that could be presented to students as models. For example:

  • Into Music resources – contain examples of soundscapes created and performed by children, with scores given in the book and soundtrack the accompanying CD;
  • Our Music resource by Elizabeth Kerr (Learning Media, 1989) – contains some excellent examples of soundscapes written and performed by prominent New Zealand musicians, such as Soundscape with Lake and River by Douglas Lilburn (Track 2); and Song by David Farquhar (Track 51) based on the poem Wild Iron by Allen Curnow (page 25, Our Music: A Teachers' Guide).
