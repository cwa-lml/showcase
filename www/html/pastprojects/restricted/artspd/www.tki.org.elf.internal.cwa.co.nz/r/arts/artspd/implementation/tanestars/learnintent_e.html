Learning intentions, knowledge, and skills

Learning intentions for students

We are learning how to:

  • invent sound-pieces by experimenting, selecting, combining, and listening to sounds we make (Strands PK, DI);
  • show ways of using contrast to make our sound-pieces more expressive and interesting (Strand DI);
  • use appropriate music vocabulary to ask questions and talk about our work (Strand CI);
  • recognise and develop a motif in music (Strands PK, DI);
  • identify some features of a Maori chant (Strand UC).

How will we know we have done these things?

We will be able to:

  • identify and describe the ways that we have chosen sounds and put them together;
  • recognise the mood the group was creating for their part of the story, and how they have shown contrasting moods;
  • recognise a 'motif' as an idea in music;
  • listen to, and recognise, a Maori chant.

Knowledge and skills

Key vocabulary

  • tone colour – sound quality, techniques of playing
  • dynamics – soft, loud sounds
  • rhythm – patterns of duration
  • texture – thin and thick layers of sound
  • melody – pitched tune
  • structural devices – contrast, repetition, variation, motif

Music knowledge and skills

Music knowledge and skills are developed in this unit through:

  • manipulating elements;
  • understanding purpose and function; and
  • developing effectiveness, by:
    • listening,
    • experimenting,
    • inventing,
    • playing,
    • improvising,
    • discussing.

Essential learning skills

The essential learning skills used in this unit are:

  • social and co-operative;
  • physical;
  • problem solving.
