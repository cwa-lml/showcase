  4. "Introduction" p.7

    Note:
    • The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum is one of the essential learning areas identified in the New Zealand Curriculum Framework
    • The curriculum includes all four disciplines - Dance, Drama, Music and Visual Arts.
    • Learning in all four disciplines is essential, in order for schools to provide curriculum that has depth and breadth, consistent with the requirements of NAG 1
    • It is expected that students will study all 4 disciplines in years 1-8 and study in at least two disciplines in years 9-10

    The document clearly signals that each discipline has "its own knowledge and means of inquiry." p.7

    With the above points in full view discuss the implications for your school-wide and classroom programmes.

  6. The Arts

    "The Arts are powerful forms of personal and social expression. They link imagination, thinking and feeling. They provide essential learning for living and develop a wide range of both general and specific skills, which are significant in many aspects of life, including employment. They are important for recreation and leisure.

    (The New Zealand Curriculum Framework Ministry of Education, 1993)

    "What strikes me is the fact that in our society, art has become something which is related only to objects and not to individuals or to life. That art is something which is specialised or which is done by experts who are artists. But couldn't' everyone's life become a work of art? From the idea that the self is not given to us, I think that there is only one practical consequence. We have to create ourselves as a work of art."

    (Rabinow, Paul (ed.) (1984).
    (The Foucault Reader. London: Penguin.pp.350-351)

    "New Zealand is a small nation in an increasingly globalised world. What is unique about us are our arts, our culture, and our heritage. In the twenty-first century, they will define us as the confident, proud, and creative peoples we are."

    (Helen Clark, May 2000)

    In the statements above, identify commonalities and different perspectives (key phrases/words) and discuss.

  10. Literacies in the Arts (OHT 3) p.10

    Developing literacies in the arts is not seen as only text based and not the sole province of Maths and English. It is a core idea within this curriculum. Literacies in this document means " the ability to communicate and interpret meaning in the arts disciplines."

    In addition:

    "Literacies in the arts require an understanding of particular cultural and practical conventions within each arts discipline." p.11
    Again this signals that skills, knowledge and understanding in one discipline do not necessarily imply the same in other disciplines.

    Paragraph 5 - makes the point that everyone "the performers, the makers and the viewers and listeners"p.11 needs an education in the arts.

    Paragraph 6 - A key message is that an arts education is a life long, enriching process for all New Zealanders, beginning with young children.

  13. Achievements Objectives (OHT 7) p.15

    There are eight levels as in all other curriculum areas (OHT 9). The mussel shell reflects a spiralling view of learning, encapsulated in the students revisiting the same material in subsequent levels but having more depth and breadth of knowledge, skills and understanding to work with it. Note that Level 1-4 Achievement Objectives in all disciplines are shown in a useful chart at the back pp 108,109.

    Note in this chart, the developmental nature of the AOs and the changing descriptors as the key indicators for teaching progression.

Matching Activity for Dance, Drama, Music and Visual Arts

Levels 1- 4 Achievement Objectives

Dance
Using the curriculum statement to help you, Identify the levels and strands (PK, DI, CI, UC) for the following achievement objectives
Students will:

    Level Strand
1. Identify and explore through movement the dance elements of body awareness, space, time, energy and relationships    
2. demonstrate an awareness of dance as part of community life.    
3. present dance and respond to their own and others' dance works within their school communities.    
4. explore through movement the dance elements of body awareness, space, time, energy and relationships.    
5. share dance through informal presentation and describe how dance expresses ideas and feelings.    
6. explore and use choreographic devices to give form to dance ideas.    
7. select, combine and use elements of dance to develop ideas.    
8. explore and describe how dance is used for different purposes in a variety of cultures    

 

Drama
Using the curriculum statement to help you, Identify the levels and strands (PK, DI, CI, UC) for the following achievement objectives
Students will:

    Level Strand
1. contribute ideas and participate in drama, using personal experiences and imagination.    
2. use elements and techniques of drama to explore dramatic conventions.    
3. share drama through informal presentation and respond to elements of drama.    
4. investigate the purposes of drama in past and present contexts and describe how communities express themselves through drama.    
5. explore elements and techniques of drama.    
6. contribute and develop ideas in drama based on personal experience, imagination, and other stimuli.    
7. present and respond to drama and identify ways in which dramatic elements, techniques and conventions combine to create meaning.    
8. investigate the purpose of drama in past and present contexts and describe how communities express themselves through drama.    

Music
Using the curriculum statement to help you, Identify the levels and strands (PK, DI, CI, UC) for the following achievement objectives
Students will:

    Level Strand
1. share music making with others through informal presentation and respond to live or recorded music.    
2. identify through focused listening and experiment with, contrasts within musical elements.    
3. identify music as part of everyday life and recognise that it serves a variety of purposes.    
4. identify through focused listening and experiment with a range of patterns, effects, sound qualities, and structural devices.    
5. invent and represent musical ideas, drawing on imagination and responding to sources of motivation.    
6. invent and represent musical ideas to express mood, using shape and contrast.    
7. share music making with others, using basic performance skills and techniques, and respond to live or recorded music.    
8. identify and investigate characteristics of music associated with particular contexts, purposes and styles in past and present cultures.    

The Visual Arts
Using the curriculum statement to help you, Identify the levels and strands (PK, DI, CI, UC) for the following achievement objectives
Students will:

    Level Strand
1. express visual ideas in response to a variety of motivations, using imagination, observation and invention with materials.    
2. explore elements and principles of the visual arts, using a variety of techniques, tools, materials, processes and procedures.    
3. apply knowledge of elements and principles to make objects and images and explore art-making conventions, using a variety of techniques, tools, materials, processes and procedures.    
4. describe ways in which objects and images can communicate stories and ideas.    
5. generate and develop visual ideas in response to a variety of motivations, using imagination, observation, and a study of artists' works.    
6. identify objects and images in everyday life and recognise that they serve a variety of purposes.    
7. investigate the purposes of objects and images in past and present cultures and identify contexts in which they were or are made, viewed and valued.    
8. explore and describe how different media influence the communication and interpretation of ideas in their own and others' work.
