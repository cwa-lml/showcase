Printmaking – Makarewa School, Invercargill

Introduction | Organisation/Planning | Content | Issues | Evaluation/Reflection

Content


enlarge image+

Over a period of several days, the class made observational drawings of Mr. Pegg's cottage from the roadside.

This drawing shows a student's attempt at using detail to capture the 'feel' of the cabbage tree with its leaning trunk and its wild hairy head of leaves.

"The real test of a print is how well I think the image is resolved - whether there is a sense of unity in its composition, whether its colour, texture, and forms are in satisfactory balance, and whether it evokes a special feeling"

Jo Ogier – artist

The same student made her drawing into this print below. This was her first experience of printmaking and it was evident to her teacher that her skills had yet to be refined. Yet, the overall effect she created through the juxtaposition of the tree and the cottage, as well as the smudged lines of the print itself, was one that conveyed a feeling of desolation and forgotten history.


enlarge image+

Dianne's class responded well to Jo Oglier's work. They loved the colours and the shapes of the nikau. Living in Southland many of the students had never seen a nikau palm before. This was the drawing focus for Jo, so Dianne had them focus on drawing local plants from their own environment such as the cabbage tree.
