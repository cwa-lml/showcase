Assessment in the Visual Arts Carol Langton, 2001
An edited excerpt from a paper presented at the 'Learning Though The Arts Conference' in Wellington, New Zealand on 10 July 2001.The paper explores three types of assessment methods that generalist teachers use to promote identity and stimulate creativity in their visual arts programme.
(http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates2_e.php)

Boys and Drama
Paper by Chris Horne about the value of drama as a tool to enhance boys' learning. It includes guidelines teachers can follow to establish a safe and structured drama environment for boys.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates8_e.php

Curriculum Update 43
This publication outlines some of the Ministry of Education's planning and strategies to support boards of trustees, principals, and teachers in the arts. It also relates the arts curriculum to secondary assessment and the National Certificate of Educational Achievement (NCEA). It includes the contact details the network of regional spokespersons that has been formed to answer any media or school questions to do with the new arts curriculum.
(http://www.tki.org.nz/r/governance/
curric_updates/curr_update43_e.php)

Dancing in the Millennium
This paper by Tina Hong National Coordinator for Dance, proposes a postmodern approach to school–based dance curriculum. The discussion explores the dance literacy approach based on a conceptual framework that conceives of dance as a literacy located within the multiliteracies of postmodernity.
(http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates1_e.php)

NCEA Music for gifted and talented students
Chris Archer, Secondary Music Adviser at the Christchurch College of Education, explains the traits of gifted students, and gives ideas and activities to help cater for them in the mainstream music classroom at NCEA levels.
(http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates6_e.php)

Teaching and Learning in Dance
Ralph Buck, University of Otago dance lecturer, first presented this paper as part of the Education and Training Symposium at World Dance Alliance 2001: Asia Pacific Dance Bridge, Singapore. It outlines his PhD research into what dance in the classroom means to primary school teachers. The paper describes the New Zealand context, Ralph's professional background, and his methodology. It presents some initial research observations, and discusses their implications for dance education in New Zealand. Since Ralph presented this paper his research has developed further. His most recent work will be presented on this site in 2003.
(http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates5_e.php)

Teaching in 3D
Merryn Dunmill, national coordinator for music, suggests research into teacher development is often based on the premise that the quality or outcome of teaching within a school-based music curriculum can be improved by understanding what teachers do and how they think. Her paper explores how improved music teaching makes for more informed students, better quality schools, and ultimately, a better society.
(http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates4_e.php)

The Arts
This is an article about the planned publication of the draft arts national curriculum statement, published in the Education Gazette in August 1998. It outlines the developments to date including the writers, research about New Zealanders and the Arts, and the writing and consultation process.
(http://www.edgazette.govt.nz/articles/show_articles.cgi?id=5420)

Vision
This paper by David Best (2000) discusses specifically why drama and in a wider context the arts are important. From his pilosophical viewpoint he offers reasons to support the arts in education while raising issues and challenging some of the current justifications for the arts in education.
(http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates3_e.php)

What drama can do for literacy
Some of the ways in which drama can contribute to more effective teaching of reading and writing are described and discussed by Rachel Roper of the Massey University College of Education. This paper was first aired in a discussion forum at the April 2003 NZADIE Conference in Auckland.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/artspd/research/updates6_e.php
