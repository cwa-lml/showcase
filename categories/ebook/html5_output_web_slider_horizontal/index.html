  • Toni went to the supermarket with Mum.

    “Please can I make some popcorn?”
    Toni asked.

    “OK,” said Mum. “You can do
    it after dinner. Your sister
    can help you.”

  • After dinner, Toni got the popping corn,
    a pot, and some butter.

    “Come on, Jo,” she called.
    “Come and help.”

    Toni’s sister came into the kitchen.

    “I’m a good popcorn maker,” she said.

  • Toni put the pot on the stove.

    She put some butter in the pot,

    and her sister turned on the gas.

    “I know how to make popcorn,”
    said Jo.

  • The butter began to melt.

    “Put the popping corn in now,” said Jo.

    Toni put two spoonfuls into the pot.

    “That won’t be enough,” said Jo.

    “Put it all in.”

  • So Toni put all the popping corn
    in the pot.

    She put on the lid and waited.

    Jo shook the pot so that
    the popping corn wouldn’t burn.

    After a while, the corn began to pop.

    Pop! Pop! Pop!

    “Yes!” cried Jo.

    “Now it’s started!”

  • Jo shook the pot again.

    Pop! Pop! Pop! Pop!

    The popcorn was jumping about.

    It was pushing on the lid.

    Pop! Pop! Pop! Pop!

  • After a few minutes, Jo said,

    “It will be ready now.”

    She lifted the lid off the pot.


  • The corn popped out of the pot
    and onto the floor.

    Pop! Pop! Pop!

    Jo squealed. She couldn’t get
    the lid back on.

    She tried to catch the popcorn
    in a bowl.

  • Soon there was popcorn everywhere.

    Toni tried to clean it up,

    but the corn went on popping.

    Pop! Pop! Pop!

  • Toni looked at the popcorn
    all over the kitchen.

    She looked at her sister
    trying to catch the popcorn,
    and she began to laugh.

    She laughed and laughed.

    “I think we made
    too much popcorn,”
    she said.

  • “I’m sorry,” said Jo.

    “Maybe I’m not so good
    at making popcorn –
    but I’m very good at eating it!”

    “Me too,” laughed Toni.
