Hotseating

  1. The person in role can, in fact, be played by many people. The teacher can place a chair to represent the character and any number of children can sit behind the chair and answer the questions directed to the character.
  2. Other children can "coach" the person in the hot seat if they have ideas for the answers.
  3. The hotseated person can ask for assistance from other participants.
  4. Other students can come and take over the seat if they have additional things to contribute.
