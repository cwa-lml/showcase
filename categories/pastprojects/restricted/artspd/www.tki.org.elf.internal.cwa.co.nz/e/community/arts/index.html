Kia ora and welcome to the news page for the arts community. We value your feedback and ideas, and invite you to contact us at thearts@tki.org.nz.

There are links to over 2000 quality-assured materials in this community. Find them by using the menu bars and the quick search facility under the arrow on the right-hand side of the page.

Douglas Lilburn - The Landscape of a New Zealand Composer
Explore this site to find out about the life and work of New Zealand composer, Douglas Lilburn. The material includes information, images, and some audio clips, and is based on a ten-part radio series produced by Radio New Zealand's Concert FM.
http://www.douglaslilburn.com/
02-05-2006

Competent Children, Competent Learners
Read the latest research in the longitudinal Competent Children, Competent Learners project which, since 1993, has tracked the development of a group of children from near five years old and in early childhood education through into secondary school.
http://www.minedu.govt.nz/index.cfm?layout=document&documentid=11105&data=l

Radio New Zealand – Mozart 250
Check out the programmes on Concert FM throughout 2006, which feature Mozart and celebrate his 250th birthday anniversary. In the current series, Mozart Moments (playing weekly on Sundays at 2pm, starting on 2 April), John Drummond looks at events in Mozart's life and how they impacted on his music.
http://www.radionz.co.nz/cfm/mozart250
28-03-2006

Radio New Zealand – Worldplay 2006
Tune-in to the National Programme on Sunday afternoons (3-4pm, during April), for the annual international season of plays from around the world. Hosted by award-winning actor Jimmy Smits, Worldplay 2006 takes as its theme “The Rules of the Game”.
http://www.radionz.co.nz/nr/programmes/sundaydrama
28-03-2006

Performing Arts Facilities in Schools
Explore this new 'Key Collection' (found on the right-hand tool bar of this page), which offers advice and guidance for schools developing new or improved facilities for dance, drama, and music programmes based on The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum. The information has been developed from the collected experiences of more than 60 New Zealand schools and many performing arts education specialists.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/facilities/
23-03-2006

Access Art: The Immersive Learning Space
Discover this exciting digital learning space for ages 12 years and up. It uses video, animation, and sound to explore ideas about when and where teenagers learn best. The site has been created and inspired by teenagers, architects, dancers, sculptors, and filmmakers.
http://www.accessart.org.uk/workshops_immersive.php
17-03-2006

Music Education Research Centre (MERC) PDF (13KB)
Read about this important new music education research centre, based at Canterbury University. The centre's first major project, funded by the Ministry of Education, is to look into ICT and the arts, examining how technology and e-learning impacts on music, dance, drama, and visual arts.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/pdfs/music_education_research.pdf
09-03-2006

Optical Illusions and Visual Phenomena
Interest your students in optical and visual illusions by pointing them in the direction of this interactive website. Read explanations of how each phenomenum works, and from where it originates.
http://www.michaelbach.de/ot/
02-03-2006

ArtEdventures: From Cave Art to Your Art – 30,000 Years of New Media
Encourage your students to explore how artists, including themselves, use art media, with this interactive site from Sanford ArtEdventures. Students  can create their own video, by selecting  from a range of clips related to using oil paint, acrylics, watercolours, markers, and coloured pencils. They upload images of their own, and add text and music to complete their video.
http://www.sanford-artedventures.com/play/caveart/index.html
02-03-2006

The Case of the Missing Object
Promote interest in sculpture, by using this interactive website from Tate Online. Students take on the role of an art detective, and solve clues about the meaning, making, and context of Henry Moore’s Recumbent Figure.
http://www.tate.org.uk/detective/mysteriousobject.htm

  Community Facilitators choice   

Arts Online – Te Hāpori o Ngā Toi
Check out the Arts Online website for:

  • online teaching resources such as units, lessons and websites.
  • professional resources such as online readings, research, case studies,
    professional associations and events.
  • all current key online resources eg. the Arts Exemplars, the Arts Unwrapped and NCEA resources.
  • Professional communication through discussion groups for each discipline.
  • An online Ask facility through which teachers can have professional and resource questions answered by an expert.
  • An Arts Teacher Resource Exchange where teachers can share and access teaching ideas and resources.
  • Fortnightly newsletters to all who subscribe to the discussion groups or provide their email address. Click here to subscribe.
    http://arts.unitec.ac.nz/

Discovering Dance – Teachers' Notes
Use these teachers' notes, which accompany the new resource Discovering Dance, designed to help students in Aotearoa New Zealand learn and understand the discipline of dance. The two DVDs and video, which schools will receive shortly, explore a range of dance genres/styles based around seven key themes originating from many different cultures. Find this resource in 'The Arts / Ngā Toi Materials', under 'Key collections' on the right-hand tool bar of this page.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/dance/discover_dance/index_e.php


New Zealand Curriculum Marautanga Project
Revisit this site to follow developments in the review of the Arts learning area. Go to What's happening - Arts, to view the draft essence statement, and the new draft curriculum achievement objectives. Navigate to the Curriculum Project Online page to participate in the discussions.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/nzcurriculum/index_e.php

Into Music 4
Use this online support material referred to in Into Music 4, a resource for teachers of years 1–13 students. It contains 13 examples from schools of music planning, including school-wide music programmes, full unit plans, and learning ideas. There is also advice on assessment in music, and planning and reviewing a school arts policy. Navigate to this resource via 'The Arts / Ngā Toi Materials', under 'Key collections' on the right-hand tool bar of this page.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/arts/music/intomusic4/

 

Software for Learning
Discover Software for Learning – a Ministry of Education initiative now on TKI. This area provides information for teachers when choosing software for learning. The first phase of the project is to provide descriptions of over 40 freeware programs that can be used to design specific tasks to meet the needs of individual students or groups of learners. Use the search facility available to find information about software to use in your learning and teaching programmes.
http://www.tki.org.nz/r/ict/software/index_e.php


Digital Conversations
This is a free videoconference programme. Every month we create opportunities for teachers and students to meet and speak with local and international experts across the curriculum. Check upcoming and previous guests http://www.digitalconversations.org.nz/guests_e.php. to be added to the mailing list and receive email notification of upcoming video conferences. If you would like your students to talk with a particular expert contact us and we will endeavour to organize this for you.

Music Association of Auckland – Composition Competition
Consider entering your students into this competition, which requires them to write a 4-8 minute piece for a quartet of any instrumental combination, including at least one voice or traditional Chinese instrument. Prizes include cash and performance opportunities. The deadline for submissions is 12 August 2006.

Arts Online Teacher Resource Exchange
Take the opportunity to contribute a resource to the Arts Online Teacher Resource Exchange, and receive payment for it. Download this information sheet (PDF 9.08KB) for more details about the specific teaching/learning areas that resources are being sought in.


Maui: One Man Against the Gods
Isaac Theatre Royal, Christchurch, 31 May to 11 June 2006
Take advantage of the three special performances for schools at 7.30pm on 30 May (final dress rehearsal), and at 12 noon on 2 and 6 June. Tickets for the schools shows are at the special discounted prices of $10 for students, and $25 for accompanying adults. A limited number of subsidised $2 tickets may be available to students from lower decile schools.

Learning Through the Arts Conference
10–12 July, Rydges, Christchurch
Looking for inspiration in dance, drama, music, visual arts? Primary and intermediate teachers take this opportunity to gain new insights for arts programmes. For registration forms or further information, email  artsconf@hotmail.com 

THE BIG SING and SOUNZ NZCF Choral Composition Award – Entry forms for 2006
Access this website for information and application brochures to enter THE BIG SING and the SOUNZ NZCF Choral Composition Award in 2006. ICHPER-SD (International Council for Health, Physical Education, Recreation, Sport and Dance) 1st Oceania Congress 2006
1–4 October 2006, Wellington.
The organisers invite teachers, academics, and people in related fields, to present at this congress. Check out the website for full details.
