Movement

Some children can be inhibited when it comes to movement – one way to work up to it is to start by allowing students to play in the realm of caricature and move towards more realistic or naturalistic roles.

It is worth working with the children before beginning the drama to assist their conscious manipulation of gestures and facial expressions:

  • Be very intentional with the shapes and they can be larger and more defined than they might be in real life.
  • Tell the students that they may need to hold the position for some time so no standing on one foot or crouching uncomfortably.
  • Encourage the children to make interesting shapes – select students who have produced good work to act as an example for the rest.
  • When groups of children are making gestures collectively suggest they think about height, grouping, how they look at each other, where they lean and where their hands are pointing – how they are in relationship to each other.
