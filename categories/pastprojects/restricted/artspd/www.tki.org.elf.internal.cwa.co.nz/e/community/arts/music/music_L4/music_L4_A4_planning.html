Level 4: Music in the Media

PLANNING AND ASSESSMENT

Key teaching and learning concepts

Media Music – music used to support other media e.g.,
Television, film, radio, computers, cellphones.

Purpose – the function underlying a piece of music, why it was composed

Jingle – musical idea or short tune used commercially for advertising purposes.

Prior learning

Students need to have:

  • heard a variety of music in different media, such as radio, television, and computers;
  • experimented with creating short ideas to suit a specific situation or purpose;
  • experienced the sharing of their ideas with others;

Suggested learning outcomes

[tip]

Students will:
  • identify features of a range of music in radio, television, and IT (PK, UC);
  • describe how the music is used in each context and identify some of the purposes it serves (UC);
  • create short musical ideas for a particular purpose (DI);
  • perform and discuss original musical ideas with others (CI).

Ideas for activities

Students can:

  • teacher and /or students compile extracts of music from radio, television, and computers. Students listen, identify, and match each quiz extract with the media context they are used for (e.g., radio = a news-on-the-hour jingle);
  • students compile a list of terms that summarise each of the 3 contexts, identifying differences, for example:
    • Radio Short in length, stand-alone – jingle
    • TV Longer in length, matched to visuals – theme tune
    • IT Very short in length – Action/event related, sometimes personalised – motif

    and similarities, for example:

    • all identify the location/event and promote the product clearly.

  • Experiment with basic song writing/lyric setting as required for media music examples that use words to get their messages across:
    • Use existing brief examples of music and rework these for a certain radio station ad or product jingle, e.g., "National Anthem" melody line / tune used to promote Channel Z radio station.
    • Create a chant with short original lyrics and some sort of rhythm accompaniment, e.g., a school chant that promotes a particular group of students, accompanied by rhythm pattern/s.
    • Improvise a product jingle using a sports chant or rap e.g., Radio Classic Hits Road Report jingle.
    • Compose a brief example of music for a new radio station or product jingle. Ensure the title/name is included, with a catchy rhythm hook that clearly identifies the station.


  • Share each of their examples/ideas from above through performance. Discuss with other students how well the musical ideas presented reflect the purpose for which they were created.

  • If available, approach / access a composer, director, or editor who works in the industry and interview or question them informally about the process. Find out about copyright and plagiarism.

  • Carry out some market research by surveying friends and family about the ads and music that "work" for them and why.

Ideas for assessment

Formative:

During the learning experiences ask the students the following so that their progress can be monitored in an ongoing way:

  • Which elements of music can be heard in each example of media music? How are they used and to what effect?
  • What is different about the duration of music excerpts used in radio, television, and computers?
  • Which similarities can you identify between music for radio and television?
  • In computer music, what type of events/happenings is music used for and why?
  • How has computer music developed from radio and television music?
  • How important is the music used in each of the various media?
  • Which elements of music are most important in each type of media music? Why do you think they are used this way?
  • What musical ideas can you create to sell your product? How can you make them more effective to market the product and make an original musical statement?

Assessment task:

"Individually, or in small groups, create a brief example of media music that advertises a product for radio or television."

Key question: To what extent has/have the student/s created an effective advertising jingle that clearly identifies the product for radio or television?

Criteria for assessment could include:

  • The advertising jingle effectively identifies and promotes the product through catchy original musical ideas.
  • The advertising jingle identifies and promotes the product through original musical ideas.
  • The advertising jingle attempts to identify the product through musical ideas.

note "original" can include changing a well-known song enough to make it "new". Discussion about plagiarism and copyright should be carried out using examples currently heard in the media where mutations of well known songs are used to open the ears and eyes (and wallets) of the audience.

Possible strategies for assessment

  • Teacher assessment based on ongoing monitoring and discussion.
  • Student /peer assessment of the effectiveness of each group's example using the criteria.
