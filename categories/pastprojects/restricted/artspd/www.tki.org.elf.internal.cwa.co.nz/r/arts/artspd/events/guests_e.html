10 November "Pasifika and Māori resources in the classroom"
Pati Umaga – a Samoan artist/musician who currently works as Youth Development Officer in the Hutt Valley. He will be speaking about Pasifika arts practices in relation to New Zealand education.
Teanau Tuiono – Te Kete Ipurangi's online editor for Pasifika and Māori education.
Victoria Esson – the team leader for the Education Team at Te Papa. She has worked at the national museum for over 3 years and has accumulated 10 years of experience working within a museum environment. As part of her role, Victoria works to develop education resources that enable education audiences engage with the exhibitions and collections of Te Papa. Many of these resources are related directly to the Māori and Pacific focused collection areas offering a wealth of opportunities that will help teachers and students develop their understanding of the Arts in context.
Janice Marriott – Learning Media. Janice will let us know about the range of resources available through Learning Media.
