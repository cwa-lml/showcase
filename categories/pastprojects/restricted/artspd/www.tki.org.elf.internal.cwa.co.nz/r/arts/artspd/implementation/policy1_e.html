Purposes of online publishing

The Arts Online uses a range of learning technologies to enhance teacher professional development. These include electronic mail (email), online communication forums, and the Internet. We publish on the Te Kete Ipurangi Internet website (www.tki.org.nz) material for educational purposes, to share models of professional development and classroom practice within the education community. This may include models of professional development delivery in The Arts, teacher planning, and evaluations. Students' schoolwork and images of students and teachers involved in activities at school or in the community may also be used to share ideas.

Images of students or teachers may include scanned, digital, or video images of them taking part in professional development, school, or class activities.

The Arts Online publishes teacher or student material online in order to:

  • support the professional development of teachers in The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum, including in the role and use of technology in society;
  • promote the professional development material in the wider education community.

Guideline 1

Arts Online will publish the material only on the Ministry of Education's website Te Kete Ipurangi – The Online Learning Centre (www.tki.org.nz) and New Zealand based websites endorsed by the Ministry of Education.

Guideline 2

The Arts Online publishes teacher or student material online for the following purposes:

  • to support the professional development of teachers in The Arts in the New Zealand Curriculum, including on the role and use of technology in society;
  • to promote the professional development material in the wider education community.

Privacy and online publishing

Teachers, parents, and students should be aware that The Arts Online cannot control who has access to the information published on the Ministry of Education's website Te Kete Ipurangi – The Online Learning Centre (www.tki.org.nz) or the other websites mentioned. In recognition of this Arts Online takes steps to safeguard the privacy of the teachers and students and to comply with the Privacy Act 1993.

Guideline 3

The Arts Online acknowledges that it cannot control who accesses the websites on which teacher and students' images or material is published or the copying, by visitors to these websites, of images of the teachers and students and their work.

Guideline 4

The Arts Online will identify teachers and students on the websites only by their first name and (students) year at school. Teachers' and students' surnames, home addresses, and telephone numbers will not be available on the websites.

Copyright and online publishing

The Arts Online may publish original material that students create at school, including artwork, such as drawings, paintings, music, dances, and dramas. Original material created by students attracts protection under the Copyright Act 1994. The students own the copyright of their own artistic and literary works.

The Arts Online will not publish works on the Internet without authorisation in the form of a copyright licence from the students.

The Arts Online will not publish online any image of a student or work they have produced without the written permission of each student's legal guardians.

Guideline 5

The Arts Online will not publish a student's image or work without written authorisation from the student's legal guardians. The Arts Online will immediately remove all material relating to a student from its website if requested by a legal guardian of the student.

Management of online publishing

The Arts Online will set the educational purpose for publishing students' work online. A student's image and schoolwork may be chosen for online publication if it meets this purpose and if The Arts Online holds the consent of the parent to publish the student's material online. The material chosen must also meet the standards required for the website on which it is to be published. For example, if the student's schoolwork is to be published on Te Kete Ipurangi – The Online Learning Centre, then it must meet the publishing standards of that website.

Guideline 6

The Arts Online will not publish material online that may defame anyone, be objectionable from a human rights point of view, be obscene, or infringe the copyright of third parties. All the student material published online will be subject to an editing process, which will include the correction of spelling and grammatical errors.

Permission sought by The Arts Online

The permission The Arts Online seeks is:

  • authorisation to publish the board of trustees', principals', teachers', or student's personal image or work online as required by the Privacy Act, and
  • a licence to publish online any copyright work of the board of trustees', principal, teacher, or student.
