Implementation

Clay sculpture unit – relationships continued

Contents | Curriculum links | Teaching sequence | Student self-evaluation

Curriculum links

Class: year 3/4

Duration: 4 weeks

Media and materials: clay and clay gear

Process: modelling

Theme: relationships

Curriculum level: 2

Curriculum achievement objectives

PK strand – Developing Practical Knowledge in the Visual Arts: Students will identify and explore elements and principles of the visual arts, using a variety of techniques, tools, materials, processes, and procedures.

DI strand – Developing Ideas in the Visual Arts: Students will develop visual ideas in response to a variety of motivations, using imagination, observation, and invention with materials.

CI strand – Communicating and Interpreting the Visual Arts: Students will describe ways in which objects and images can communicate stories and ideas.

UC strand – Understanding the Visual Arts in Context: Students will experience, and identify, objects and images in everyday life, and recognise that they serve a variety of purposes.

Cross-curricula links

  • Technology
  • Social studies

Teaching and learning focus

Elements covered: texture, form, space.

Principles covered: balance, pattern.

Essential skills used

  • Communication
  • Information
  • Problem solving
  • Physical

Relationships: individual

Context: European and other cultures

Specific learning outcomes
Students will be able to:

  • identify and use a variety of tools in the making of their own clay model;
  • develop ideas for their sculpture in response to discussion, observation, and experimentation with clay
  • share ideas about the methods used to portray relationships and emotions in their own and other's work;
  • recognise the art works of Iosefa Leo by their distinguishing features.

Resources and equipment

  • Ministry of Education (2001) Sculpture: Exploring the Visual Arts in Years 1–6 booklet and posters.
  • Earthenware clay.
  • Clay tools, such as toothbrushes, clay knives, texture- and pattern-making objects, small water dishes; damp cloths or sponges.
  • Grey paper to work the clay on.
  • Examples of clay models.
  • Pictures of adults with children/pets.
