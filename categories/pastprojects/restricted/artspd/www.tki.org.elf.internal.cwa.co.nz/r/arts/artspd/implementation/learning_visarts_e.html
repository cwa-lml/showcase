Learning in the Visual Arts

Use this page to inform your teacher planning for the visual arts.

Students need to be taught visual arts concepts and they need to develop practical art skills in order to convey and receive visual meanings. The visual arts are richly taught when based upon a context. As students develop ideas within this context, they use practical knowledge and develop skills to create art objects and images to express ideas. This is the basis of the four strands.

Developing Practical Knowledge in the Visual Arts

This strand is about developing practical knowledge and skills with media techniques and processes:

      Elements
  • line

  • point

  • tone

  • texture

  • colour

  • form/mass

  • shape

  • space

       Principles
  • balance

  • harmony

  • rhythm

  • tension

  • contrast

       Forms of art making
  • 2D (two-dimensional art), e.g., photography, painting, printmaking, graphic design, drawing

  • 3D (three-dimensional art), e.g., sculpture, product design

  • TB (time-based art), e.g., kinetic art, animation, video, film, performance art

Developing Ideas in the Visual Arts

This strand is about communicating an intention:

  • observe

  • imagine

  • invent

  • respond

  • reflect

  • source

  • organise
  • conceptualise

  • express

  • clarify

  • regenerate

  • problem-solve

Communicating and Interpreting in the Visual Arts

This strand is about considering the art work itself.

Learn:

  • how art works are structured

  • what meanings they have

In practical and theoretical studies:

  • analyse

  • interpret sign and symbol systems

  • respond

  • evaluate meanings

Consider how this learning can be applied to creating art works.


Understanding the Visual Arts in Context

This strand is about considering the circumstances giving rise to the work.

Exploring the purposes and contexts and value of the visual arts in society:

  • in public and private settings

  • in styles and genres from past and present cultures

Understanding that visual cultures reflect the nature of societies.


Prepared by Ann Brodie – National Coordinator for the Visual Arts.
