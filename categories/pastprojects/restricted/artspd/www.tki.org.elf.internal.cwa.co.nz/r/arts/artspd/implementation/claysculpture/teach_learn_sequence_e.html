Clay sculpture unit – relationships continued

Teaching and learning sequence

Description
Make a model that shows an adult and child/pet together. Include appropriate props, and try to show the relationship between the figures by the way they are placed and modelled in relation to each other.

Motivation (UC and CI strands)
Look at example/s of sculpture/s.

  1. Discuss with class what makes three-dimensional artworks different from two-dimensional artworks. Students then talk about similar objects found in their homes, and the purposes of these objects.
  2. Look at, and discuss, the MOE Sculpture posters showing Iosefa Leo's work. To facilitate the discussion, use the examples of questions from the Sculpture booklet (pages 9–10) and information about the artist (pages 6–8).

Generating ideas (PK and DI strands)
Refer to the Sculpture booklet, pages 11–12, for more information about this activity.

  1. Ask students about the ways families and friends might group or sit together.
  2. Use magazine/newspaper pictures and life models to make observations of different family groupings.
  3. Explore the properties of clay by making several two-minute models of adults and children/pets grouped together. Focus on form, balance, and the way the relationship between the figures is portrayed.
  4. Explore ways different clay tools can be used to add detail.

Making the sculpture (PK strand)

  1. Students make the sculpture, using the process outlined in the MOE Sculpture booklet, page 12. Remember to keep joins strong, and to check for cracking and overworking of clay.
  2. Allow 2–3 weeks for models to dry, and then have them fired. (Sometimes a local pottery club, or local intermediate or secondary school with a kiln, will do the firing for a small fee.)
  3. After firing, students can choose to:
    • leave their sculptures in its natural state;
    • paint it;
    • glaze/oxide the sculpture, and then have it re-fired.

Reflection (CI strand)
Ask the students to:

  • display their work to the class and talk about – see Sculpture booklet, page 13;
  • complete the self-evaluation form.

Extension (UC strand)
Have the class make group charts showing everyday things made from clay, using pictures from magazines and newspapers. (This relates to "objects and images in everyday life" in the UC Achievement Objective.)
