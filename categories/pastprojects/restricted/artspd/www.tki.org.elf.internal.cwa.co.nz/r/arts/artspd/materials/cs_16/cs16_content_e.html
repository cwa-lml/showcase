Establishing a School of Arts at Tauranga Boys' College

Content

The School of Arts at Tauranga Boys' College has considered new pathways for student learning and greater alignment with 'real-world' arts practices and career opportunities. Select this link to view an example of their programme.

For timetabling details and how the school scheduled their Arts programme please contact the school directly.

The content of programmes in the Arts includes the following aspects:

Community links

At Tauranga Boys' College the students and community are working in two ways.

  1. The students are going out to the community and developing combined projects with community arts groups.
  2. Community experts are coming into the school.

Community projects become sources of motivation, when career pathways are clearly aligned with classroom practices. Students are able to learn and apply their skills, knowledge, and understandings of arts practices in real world experiences.

Collaborative arts links

Within the school programmes, newly evolving cross-curricular activities are proving very popular and rewarding for students. For example, at year 10 there is a collaborative cross-arts iMovie course where students create video animations with soundtracks, burn their creations onto CD for assessment purposes and upload selected examples onto the school website. The courses have developed during 2004 to include Flash as a higher technical multimedia option. The new software iStopmotion, is proving very successful as it allows students to put their work in this program, directly into iMovie for editing. It is important to note that the school site license for copyright 'keeps APRA happy' and covers school-based use of copyright materials such as recorded music.

Special needs students

Students with special needs are catered for in the Arts programmes. They are integrated into mainstream classes but with specific IPs (individual programmes) that suit their particular needs for example, making CD-ROMs with Garageband software in music courses. Teachers work with the special needs department at the school to support students' goals.

Gifted and talented students

Classes at Tauranga Boys' College are banded in the school with specific accelerate classes and programmes that are adapted to suit needs and abilities of all students. Gifted and talented students are motivated and excited by their ability to develop arts practices, for example, creative problem-solving as they use technologies like Flash software.

"The students are pushed outside the square in their multi-media presentations."

Matt Edwards
Multimedia ICT teacher
Learning initiatives
  • Tauranga Boys' College provides motivated students with many additional opportunities to extend their learning in the Arts. Arts teachers provide time in school holidays and during weekends for students to come in for extension work. Examples of such extension projects have seen senior and junior students funded to visit WETA film design studios, Art workshops, secondary schools orchestra and jazz festivals, music composition and conducting master classes with leading music artists.
  • Another initiative offered by the School of Arts is an annual scholarship offered to support a worthy student to attend tertiary courses in the arts.
  • In 2005 Tauranga city hosts a visual arts workshop held by established artists, and to which school students will be funded to attend. This funding is courtesy of money raised by Tauranga Boys' College for arts education.
